Etymology & Story

Zmirah (זמירה) comes from the Hebrew root z-m-r (ז-מ-ר), meaning "to sing" or "to make music." The word appears in Psalms and ancient Hebrew poetry. In modern Hebrew, zemer means a song, and zmirah is the act of singing -- the living, breathing expression of music itself.

The "-ah" ending gives it a feminine, flowing quality that feels both ancient and fresh. It rolls off the tongue in English ("zmee-rah") and is instantly recognizable to any Hebrew speaker.

  • Biblical root: Psalms, Song of Songs
  • Meaning: "Song / singing / musical expression"
  • Pronunciation: zmee-RAH (stress on last syllable)
  • Easy to spell in English, natural in Hebrew

Brand Rationale

Zmirah positions the platform as the act of music itself -- not just a tool, but the song that connects teacher to student, performer to audience, musician to community. It works across every user persona:

  • Students: "My Zmirah" = my musical journey
  • Teachers: "On Zmirah" = where I teach
  • Parents: "Check Zmirah" = the music app
  • Event seekers: "Book on Zmirah" = find musicians

The Z gives it a sharp, memorable start (like Zoom, Zara). The -ah ending connects to Hebrew tradition while sounding modern globally.

Etymology & Story

Niguno (ניגונו) literally means "his melody" or "his tune" in Hebrew -- from nigun (ניגון), one of the most spiritually charged words in Jewish music. A nigun is a wordless melody, passed from generation to generation, transcending language.

The possessive suffix "-o" (his/its) makes it personal: this is YOUR nigun, your personal melody. In Hasidic tradition, every soul has its own nigun. The lowercase branding gives it a modern, approachable, tech-startup feel (like spotify, airbnb).

  • Root: nigun (ניגון) = melody, tune, musical motif
  • "-o" suffix = possessive (his/its melody)
  • Deep Hasidic/Kabbalistic resonance
  • Lowercase style: modern tech identity

Brand Rationale

Niguno captures the idea that every person in the music ecosystem has their own melody -- their own role, their own contribution. It is deeply, authentically Israeli while having zero pronunciation problems in English.

  • The "n" lowercase logo mark is clean and iconic
  • Works as a verb: "niguno it" (find music on niguno)
  • Spiritual depth for those who know; cool sound for those who don't
  • All 5 TLDs available = clean slate

Teal/cyan palette evokes clarity, flow, and digital innovation while remaining warm and inviting.

Etymology & Story

Kinora (כינורא) is derived from kinnor (כינור), the biblical lyre -- the instrument King David played. The kinnor is THE symbol of Jewish music, appearing throughout the Bible and immortalized in the phrase "kinnor David" (David's harp).

The "-a" ending transforms it from the instrument itself into something larger: a place, a concept, a platform. It follows the Hebrew pattern of creating abstract nouns (like "menorah" from "ner"). In Aramaic, "kinora" was actually used as a variant of kinnor.

  • Root: kinnor (כינור) = David's lyre/harp
  • Most iconic musical instrument in Jewish history
  • "-a" suffix = place/concept (Aramaic pattern)
  • Strong, regal sound: KIN-or-ah

Brand Rationale

Kinora carries the weight of 3,000 years of Jewish music in just 6 letters. The kinnor appears on Israeli coins, in the national anthem, and in the cultural consciousness of every Israeli. This name says: "We are the continuation of that musical tradition."

  • Instant recognition for Hebrew speakers
  • The "K" gives it a strong, tech-friendly start
  • All-caps styling creates a bold, confident brand
  • .co.il available (the #1 priority domain)

Warm amber/gold palette channels the golden strings of the kinnor and premium Israeli craftsmanship.

Etymology & Story

Tslilo (צלילו) comes from tslil (צליל), the Hebrew word for "sound" or "tone" -- the most fundamental unit of music. Every note, every chord, every melody begins with a single tslil. The "-o" possessive suffix means "its sound" or "his sound."

The Ts (tsadi) at the start gives it a distinctly Hebrew character that is impossible to confuse with any English word. It is authentic, specific, and proudly Israeli. The sound "tsli-lo" has a rhythmic, musical quality itself.

  • Root: tslil (צליל) = sound, tone, note
  • The fundamental building block of all music
  • "-o" possessive: "its sound" -- the platform's voice
  • Rhythmic pronunciation: tsli-LO

Brand Rationale

Tslilo is the most audaciously Hebrew option -- it leans into the uniqueness of the language rather than away from it. The "Ts" sound is distinctly Semitic and impossible to mistake for any other brand. For the Israeli market, this is a feature, not a bug.

  • 100% unique: no brand anywhere sounds like this
  • The "sound/tone" meaning covers ALL music activities
  • Represents the platform as the "sound" of the ecosystem
  • All 5 domains available
