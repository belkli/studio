Twango

"Twang your way to music mastery"
twang (guitar string sound) + go (movement/action)

The sound a guitar string makes when plucked -- TWANG -- fused with GO, the universal word for action. Short, punchy, impossible to forget. Kids love saying it. Works perfectly in Hebrew (טוואנגו) and English. Evokes immediate musicality and energy.

Bz

Beatzy

"Find your beat, find your teacher"
beat (rhythm/pulse) + -zy (playful suffix, like "easy")

The rhythmic core of all music -- the BEAT -- made playful and approachable with the "-zy" suffix (echoing "easy" and "breezy"). 6 letters, instantly musical. The "zy" ending makes it sound fun and casual, perfect for kids saying "I'm on Beatzy!" Hebrew: ביטזי -- rolls off the tongue.

Moo

Meloodoo

"Where melodies come to play"
melody + "oo" (playful doubling, like "voodoo" / "wahoo")

Takes "melody" and transforms it with a playful double-O ending that kids adore saying. The "oo" sound is universally fun across languages -- Hebrew speakers love it (מלודו). 7 letters, 3 syllables, rhythmic and bouncy. Sounds like something between a melody and a happy exclamation. The double-O creates a visual logo opportunity.

Hz

Humzik

"Hum it, play it, live it"
hum (musical humming) + muzik/zik (Hebrew: music = muzika/zemer)

Fuses the universal act of humming -- the most natural musical expression -- with "zik", a fragment of the Hebrew word "muzika" (music). This is the most Hebrew-native name in the set. 6 letters, punchy, and carries a dual-language charm. Israeli kids will get it instantly. Even sounds like a frequency unit (Hz), adding a techy-musical double meaning.

"Sing it. Learn it. Live it."

The most universal musical act -- singing -- made delightfully casual with the "-zy" suffix. Immediately communicates "music" to anyone in any language. 6 letters, 2 syllables, bouncy and fun. Kids will say "Let's go to Singzy!" Hebrew: סינגזי -- natural, easy to pronounce. Strong verb-based brand name that implies action and joy.
