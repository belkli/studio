## 3. Key Findings

### KF-1: Price Range Across Conservatoriums

The market spans a wide range:

- **30-min individual lesson:** ₪310–₪380/month (rare; Ashkelon and Beer Sheva only)
- **45-min individual lesson:** ₪460–₪662/month (Beer Sheva ₪460 – Givat'ayim ₪662)
- **60-min individual lesson:** ₪495–₪798/month (Herzliya ₪495 – Givat'ayim ₪798)
- **Group lesson:** ₪120–₪280/month

The Givat'ayim pricing is the highest in the corpus, likely reflecting a private-style urban operator. Beer Sheva and Herzliya are municipality-linked and therefore more regulated/lower.

### KF-2: VAT is Universally Absent

**Not a single document in the corpus discloses VAT.** All prices are stated without indicating whether they are VAT-inclusive or VAT-exclusive. This is a systematic PDPPA/consumer protection gap across the sector. For Lyriosa, this means all conservatoriums currently violate Section 1.2 of the Consumer Protection Law (price display requirements).

### KF-3: No 14-Day Cooling-Off Period in Any Document

None of the 13 documents mentions the Consumer Protection Law's 14-day cooling-off right for distance contracts (Section 14ג). The closest is Muzichli's 75%/25% refund after 1/2 lessons, and Arava's trial period until 1/11. These are discretionary — they are not legally compliant with the statutory right.

### KF-4: Cancellation Mechanisms Are Mostly Offline

- **Online/email cancellation present:** Muzichli (email to Rotem), Givatayim (email), Herzliya Menagen (email)
- **Written letter to secretariat required:** Bat Yam, Eshkol
- **Phone-only mentioned:** Ashkelon (clause 4.3: "הודעה תהיה טלפונית בלבד")
- **No cancellation allowed after certain date:** All documents share a March/April hard cutoff — this is likely enforceable under the conservatorium's standard but may conflict with the Consumer Protection Law's right to cancel within 14 days of any transaction

### KF-5: Scholarship Criteria Are Opaque

All documents mention scholarship/discount committees but none provides explicit income thresholds, percentage reduction ranges, or appeals processes. Givat'ayim is the most explicit: "income test for Givat'ayim residents only." Beer Sheva references the Ministry of Education scholarship fund separately. Ashdod references an association scholarship program.

### KF-6: Photo/Video Consent Varies Significantly

- **Best practice (Ashkelon, Ashdod):** Separate clause with explicit opt-in or opt-out checkbox for photos/videos; marketing purposes clearly stated
- **Minimal (Beer Sheva, Herzliya):** Checkbox on registration form with implicit consent
- **Absent (Bat Yam, Eshkol, Arava):** No mention of photo/video consent in bylaws

This is a significant PDPPA gap. The `VIDEO_RECORDING` ConsentType in Lyriosa's `types.ts` addresses this correctly, but needs to be wired to the UI with separate per-purpose consent.

### KF-7: Parent/Guardian Identity Collection Is Inconsistent

- All registration forms collect parent name, phone, and email
- None explicitly states that the parent is signing as legal guardian for a minor
- None differentiates consent for a minor vs. an adult self-enrollee
- The "I declare I have read and agree" language is identical for both adults and parents registering children

This is the most critical legal gap for Lyriosa's parental consent differentiation requirement.

### KF-8: Herzliya Bylaws Have the Strongest Data Governance

The Herzliya (תקנון-קונס-תשפד.pdf) document is the only one with structured sections including: lesson scheduling, makeup policy, distance learning contingency (COVID/security), and formal stopping procedure via email only. It references the conservatorium's website for the holiday calendar.

### KF-9: Privacy Policy PDF is a Municipal Template (Beer Sheva / OneCity)

The `Privacy-Policy.pdf` is a **municipal OneCity platform template** for Beer Sheva City Hall (updated 30.10.2025), not a conservatorium-specific document. It references Amendment 13 to the Privacy Protection Law and the 2025 Privacy Regulations (תקנות הגנת הפרטיות (שימוש במידע אישי ומסירתו), התשפ"ה-2025) — the most up-to-date legal framework. Key elements:
- DPO appointment required (new under Amendment 13)
- Cookie consent banner mentioned
- 30-day DSAR response (extendable to 60 days)
- Sub-processors listed conceptually (hosting, payment, cloud)
- Data transfer outside Israel requires adequate safeguards
- No specific sub-processor names listed (still a gap)

### KF-10: Ashdod Document Has the Most Legally Complete Structure

The `1754911083.3447.pdf` (Akadema Ashdod) has the closest structure to a complete legal agreement:
- Numbered clauses
- Explicit photo/video consent opt-in AND opt-out
- SMS/email consent with clear limitation to service-only communications (not advertising)
- Clear late payment consequences
- Scholarship program reference
- Parent signature with date and ID number

This document is the best template reference for Lyriosa's standard registration agreement.
