Why TingTing

The universal sound of a triangle or bell chime. Doubled syllables are scientifically proven to be more memorable (TikTok, Lulu, BonBon). Kids naturally gravitate to repeating sounds — it's how language begins.

Sound Profile

  • Bright, metallic bell tone
  • 2 identical syllables = instant recall
  • Works in Hebrew: טינג-טינג
  • Natural music class association
  • Easy for ages 5-95 to pronounce

Brand Identity

  • Warm gold + deep purple palette
  • Bell/triangle icon motif
  • Playful yet trustworthy
  • Works for both kids & institutions

Klingli
Music that sparkles
קלינגלי
Make it Ring

Why Klingli

From the German "klingelingling" — the sound of a ringing bell or chime. The "-li" suffix is a Swiss/Yiddish diminutive, making it endearing and small-scale, perfect for children. Feels like "little ringing one."

Sound Profile

  • Sparkling bell/chime resonance
  • The "kl-" onset = crisp, clear attack
  • "-gli" ending = playful, diminutive
  • Hebrew "kuf-lamed" combo is natural
  • 3 syllables with a skip-like rhythm

Brand Identity

  • Emerald + sky blue = fresh, bright
  • Bell/chime icon with sparkle motif
  • Whimsical yet professional
  • European classical music heritage feel
klingli.co.il klingli.com

DinDong
Music opens every door
דין-דונג
Ring the Bell

Why DinDong

The universal doorbell chime — and the metaphor is perfect: "music opens every door." The alternating vowels (i/o) create a natural two-note interval, like a real door chime plays a major third. Every child knows this sound.

Sound Profile

  • Universal two-note chime
  • Vowel alternation (i/o) = musical interval
  • Hebrew: דין-דונג — very natural
  • Warm, welcoming, inviting sound
  • Built-in brand jingle potential

Brand Identity

  • Warm red-to-orange gradient = energy + warmth
  • Doorbell/welcome motif
  • "Come on in" feeling for parents
  • Strong association with beginnings/openings
dindong.co.il dindong.com

Why TrimTram

The rhythmic "tr-" consonant cluster mimics a drum roll or a train chugging along tracks. The vowel shift (i to a) gives it forward momentum — like music always pushing to the next beat. It's the sound of rhythm itself.

Sound Profile

  • Drum roll / percussion attack
  • "Tr-" onset = energy, forward motion
  • Vowel alternation (i/a) = rhythmic pattern
  • Hebrew "tet-resh" combo is strong
  • Feels like a catchy drum pattern: trim-TRAM

Brand Identity

  • Royal blue + violet gradient = dynamic
  • Drum/percussion motif, teal accents
  • Energetic, rhythmic brand personality
  • Appeals to percussionists & rhythm-focused
